# AI WRITING ANTI-PATTERNS

Structural patterns discovered through iterative evaluation of AI-generated fiction.
These survive prompt engineering and word-list detection. They are structural, not lexical.

Adapted from [NousResearch/autonovel](https://github.com/NousResearch/autonovel)'s ANTI-PATTERNS.md.

---

## 1. THE OVER-EXPLAIN
The narrator restates what a scene already showed. A character's hands shake, then
the narrator adds: "He was afraid." Cut the explanation. Trust the image.

## 2. TRIADIC LISTING
AI defaults to groups of three. "X. Y. Z." More than 2 per chapter is a pattern.
Fix: use two, or five, or one.

## 3. NEGATIVE-ASSERTION REPETITION
"He did not look back." "He did not think about the room." Max 1 per chapter.

## 4. CATALOGING-BY-THINKING
"He thought about X. He thought about Y. He thought about Z."
Fix: the thought itself as a fragment, a physical action, or dialogue.

## 5. THE SIMILE CRUTCH
"the way X did Y" — 4-8 times per chapter. Max 2 per chapter.

## 6. SECTION BREAK AS RHYTHM CRUTCH
Max 2 `---` breaks per chapter. Force continuous prose for everything else.

## 7. PARAGRAPH LENGTH UNIFORMITY
AI paragraphs cluster at 4-6 sentences. Include 1-2 sentence paragraphs for impact
and 6+ sentence paragraphs for building.

## 8. PREDICTABLE EMOTIONAL ARCS
Include one moment per chapter that surprises: wrong thing said, emotion arriving
before its trigger, a beat interrupting another beat.

## 9. REPETITIVE CHAPTER ENDINGS
No two chapters end with the same structural move.

## 10. BALANCED ANTITHESIS IN DIALOGUE
"I'm not saying X. I'm saying Y." / "Not X, but Y." / "There's a difference."
No two characters should share this sentence structure.

## 11. DIALOGUE AS WRITTEN PROSE
Characters speak in complete, polished sentences. Fix: false starts, interruptions,
trailing off, saying the wrong word. At least one imperfect line per scene.

## 12. SCENE-SUMMARY IMBALANCE
70%+ of each chapter should be in-scene (moment by moment). Summary is for
time compression only.

---

## EVALUATION NOTES

These patterns require either:
1. **Adversarial editing** — ask a judge to cut 500 words and classify cuts
2. **Comparative ranking** — head-to-head matchups (Swiss-style Elo)
3. **Sentence-level grading** — flag STRONG / FINE / WEAK / CUT

Standard 1-10 scoring collapses to a 2-point band. Avoid absolute scoring for revision.
